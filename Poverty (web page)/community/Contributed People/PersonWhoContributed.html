Bill Gates & Melinda Gates

For years, Bill and Melinda Gates have been powerful leaders in the fight against poverty. The Gates Foundation, which focuses on global health and development, was founded in 2000 and has now handed over $50 billion to assist efforts to improve health care, education, and the environment.

Oprah Winfrey

For more than a decade, Oprah Winfrey has advocated for a great education in the United States and around the world.

Ban Ki-moon

Ban Ki-moon led efforts to set the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), the first of which is to end poverty, in 2015, while serving as UN Secretary-General.

Malala Yousafzai

Malala Yousafzai, a Nobel Peace Prize winner, is a fervent advocate for gender equality and education, both of which are critical to breaking the cycle of poverty.

Majora Carter

Majora Carter, who is regarded as an environmental justice hero, has advocated for green spaces and improved access to decent health care in the South Bronx and other underserved neighbourhoods around the country.

Abisoye Ajayi-Akinfolarin

Abisoye Ajayi-Akinfolarin, a computer programmer from Lagos, Nigeria, and the creator of Pearls Africa Foundation, a social venture that teaches girls living in poverty to code, recognized as a CNN Hero in September.

Bryan Stevenson

Bryan Stevenson is a lawyer and educator who has spent decades working to reform the US justice system. Stevenson launched the Equal Justice Initiative, which has assisted in the release of more than 140 persons who were unfairly convicted and were on death row.

Reverend William Barber II

Reverend William Barber II has committed his life to combat inequality and injustice via nonviolent means, following in the footsteps of Martin Luther King Jr.

Sonita Alizadeh

Sonita Alizadeh is a rapper and activist who utilizes her platform to promote public awareness about child marriage, a destructive practice that affects 12 million girls around the world and deprives them of their basic rights to health, education, and safety.
